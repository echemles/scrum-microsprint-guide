02 / Roles

The Team

A Scrum Team is small (10 or fewer). Three clear roles, no hierarchy.

👑

Product Owner

Decides WHAT to build

Click to learn more

Product Owner

  • Owns the Product Backlog (master TODO list)
  • Prioritizes what's most valuable
  • Represents the customer/stakeholders
  • Says "yes" or "no" to what gets built
💻

Developers

Build the thing

Click to learn more

Developers

  • Self-organizing — they decide HOW to work
  • Create the product increment each sprint
  • Cross-functional — have all needed skills
  • Hold each other accountable
⚡

Scrum Master

Keeps the process running

Click to learn more

Scrum Master

  • Facilitates all Scrum events
  • Removes blockers and impediments
  • Coaches the team on Scrum practices
  • Serves the team — a leader, not a boss
03 / Heartbeat

The Sprint

Sprints are the heartbeat of Scrum — fixed-length cycles where ideas become value. Typically 1-4 weeks. Our microsprints: 1 hour.

0:00 – 0:05

Sprint Planning

Set a Sprint Goal. Pick tasks from the backlog. Answer: "What can we do this hour?"

0:05 – 0:50

Do The Work

Heads down. Build, create, execute. Move tasks from TODO to DONE.

0:50 – 0:55

Sprint Review

Show what you built. Get feedback. "Here's what we accomplished."

0:55 – 1:00

Retrospective

What went well? What to improve? Adapt for the next sprint.

04 / Daily Ritual

The Standup

A 5-minute check-in. Three questions. That's it. Stand up to keep it short.

1

What did I do?

Since the last standup

2

What will I do next?

Before the next standup

3

Any blockers?

What's in my way?

05 / Artifacts

What You Track

Three living documents that keep everyone aligned.

📜

Product Backlog

The master TODO list

An ordered list of everything that could be built. The Product Owner prioritizes it. Items at the top are the most important.

Commitment: Product Goal
📋

Sprint Backlog

This sprint's TODO list

The tasks selected for this sprint, plus a plan for how to deliver them. Owned by the Developers.

Commitment: Sprint Goal
📦

Increment

The finished work

A usable piece of the product delivered at the end of each sprint. Must meet the Definition of Done.

Commitment: Definition of Done

07 / Foundation

Scrum Values

Five values that make everything work.

🎯

Commitment

Commit to achieving the sprint goal and supporting each other.

🔍

Focus

Focus on the work of the sprint and the team's goals.

🔓

Openness

Be open about the work and the challenges you face.

🤝

Respect

Respect each other as capable, independent people.

⚔

Courage

Have the courage to do the right thing and tackle tough problems.

08 / Cheat Sheet

Quick Reference

Roles

Product Owner Decides what to build, prioritizes backlog
Developers Self-organizing team that builds the product
Scrum Master Facilitates process, removes blockers

Events

Sprint Fixed-length cycle (1-4 weeks, or 1 hour in microsprints)
Sprint Planning Kick off: set the goal, pick the work
Daily Standup 15-min daily check-in (5 min in microsprints)
Sprint Review Demo what was built, get feedback
Retrospective Reflect: what worked, what to improve

Artifacts

Product Backlog Ordered master list of all work
Sprint Backlog Work selected for current sprint + plan
Increment Usable output delivered each sprint

Key Concepts

Sprint Goal The single objective for the sprint
Definition of Done Shared standard for "finished"
Empiricism Decide based on what you observe, not theory
Self-Managing Team decides who does what, when, how
